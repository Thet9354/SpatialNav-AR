# SpatialNav Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** August 2026

SpatialNav is built for people who deserve to know exactly what an app that uses their camera does with what it sees. The answer is simple:

## What SpatialNav collects

**Nothing.** SpatialNav has no server, no user accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party SDKs. Nothing you do in the app is transmitted anywhere, ever. The app functions entirely offline.

## How the camera is used

SpatialNav uses the camera and LiDAR sensor to sense the space around you in real time — detecting obstacles, hazards, and everyday objects. Camera frames are processed on your device by Apple's ARKit, Vision, and CoreML frameworks and are **discarded immediately after processing**. They are never stored, never uploaded, and never leave your device.

When the app recognizes a person nearby, that detection is announced to you and discarded like any other frame. No images or information about people are retained.

## What is stored on your device

- **Saved spaces**: 3D maps of rooms you choose to save, so the app can recognize them when you return.
- **Saved items**: names, last known positions, and visual fingerprints of items you register.
- **Your settings**: feedback style, speech rate, and similar preferences.

All of this stays on your device. Saved maps and items are encrypted at rest using iOS data protection (the encryption key is derived from your device passcode). Deleting the app deletes all of it.

## Permissions

- **Camera** — required for all sensing. SpatialNav does not function without it.

That is the only permission SpatialNav requests.

## Children's privacy

SpatialNav collects no data from anyone, including children.

## Changes to this policy

If a future version of SpatialNav ever changes any of the above (for example, optional iCloud sync of saved spaces), this policy will be updated before that version ships, and the app's App Store privacy label will be updated to match.
